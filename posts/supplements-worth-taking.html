Magnesium Glycinate 400mg

Minerals
FormGlycinate (chelated)
Dose400mg elemental
TimingEvening

Magnesium deficiency is common in people eating modern diets, and glycinate is the form best absorbed without the digestive side effects that oxide or citrate forms often cause. It's involved in over 300 enzymatic processes. The glycinate chelation means a higher proportion actually reaches the bloodstream — which matters when most supplements are poorly absorbed to begin with.

Worth it because
  • Glycinate form has superior absorption vs oxide
  • No laxative effect at standard doses
  • Wide evidence base across multiple functions
The catch
  • Takes several weeks of consistent use to notice effects
  • Pricier than magnesium oxide alternatives

The NHS recommends vitamin D supplementation for everyone in the UK from October to March due to insufficient sunlight. D3 is the more bioavailable form versus D2. K2 as MK-7 is paired because it directs calcium to bones rather than arteries — the combination is better supported than D3 alone. One of the few supplements with a genuine public health recommendation behind it in the UK context.

Worth it because

  • Widely deficient in UK population

Creatine Monohydrate (5g daily)

Performance
FormMonohydrate
Daily Dose5g
LoadingNot required

Creatine monohydrate has more peer-reviewed research behind it than almost any other supplement. It replenishes phosphocreatine stores, which directly supports short-burst, high-intensity output. More recent research also explores cognitive benefits. Monohydrate is the original and best-studied form — there is no meaningful evidence that more expensive forms (ethyl ester, HCl) outperform it. The cheapest version works as well as the premium one.

Worth it because

  • Very inexpensive per serving
